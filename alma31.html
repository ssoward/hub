At a glance

Key themes

True vs. false prayer

The repetitive, prideful prayers of the Zoramites contrasted with Alma's heartfelt plea for the poor and afflicted.

Power of the word

Alma's faith in the power of the word to change hearts — demonstrated through his missionary approach to the apostate Zoramites.

Dangers of pride

The Rameumptom as the ultimate expression of spiritual pride and separation from God and from one another.

Compassion for the afflicted

Alma's heart breaks for those who are excluded and cast out — Christ-like compassion in the midst of apostasy.

Featured verse

Alma 31:5

And now, as the preaching of the word had a great tendency to lead the people to do that which was just — yea, it had had more powerful effect upon the minds of the people than the sword, or anything else, which had happened unto them — therefore Alma thought it was expedient that they should try the virtue of the word of God.

This verse establishes the central theme of the chapter: the power of God's word to transform hearts and minds more effectively than force or coercion.
