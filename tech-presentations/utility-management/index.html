ACH is the backbone of U.S. bill payment — batched and cleared through the Federal Reserve's ACH network, typically settling in 1–3 business days.

ACH is the dominant rail by both count and dollar volume, and extremely reliable. The tradeoff vs. cards is speed — 1–3 day settlement instead of instant — which the next slide weighs against cost.

The core economic argument. At portfolio scale, moving collections from cards to ACH cuts payment costs 70–90%, and automation removes most manual handling. The bar chart makes the gap visceral: ACH is a rounding error next to card fees.
Payments · Operations

Benefits, challenges & solutions

Benefits

  • Low, flat per-transaction cost
  • Automated disbursement & collection
  • NACHA / CTX file generation
  • Automated SFTP upload to banks
  • Real-time status tracking · multi-bank

Challenges → solutions

  • Returns & retries → status tracking
  • Reconciliation → unique-ID matching
  • Compliance → NACHA formatting
  • Scale → batch-file automation
  • Settlement lag → predictable 1–3 day windows
The hard parts of ACH are returns handling and reconciliation. The platform's answer is unique-ID matching and NACHA-formatted batch files auto-uploaded via SFTP, with multi-bank support so a large portfolio isn't tied to one institution.
