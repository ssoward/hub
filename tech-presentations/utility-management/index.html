Orientation

What is utility management?

Property managers rarely bill utilities directly. A specialized platform sits between the utility providers and the residents — capturing invoices, allocating cost, generating resident statements, collecting payment, and reconciling it all back to the ledger.

The core challenge: one utility invoice fans out into many resident bills, then must fold back into a single, auditable reconciliation — at the scale of thousands of properties.

Frame the domain before the details. The platform is the intermediary between utility companies and residents at multi-family properties. The recurring theme of the whole deck is fan-out (one invoice → many bills) and fold-back (many payments → one reconciliation).

ACH is the backbone of U.S. bill payment. Transactions are batched and cleared through the Federal Reserve's ACH network, typically settling in 1–3 business days.

Context for why ACH matters: it's the dominant rail by both count and dollar volume, and it's extremely reliable. The tradeoff versus cards is speed — 1–3 day settlement instead of instant — which the next slide quantifies against cost.

Payments · Operations

Benefits, challenges & solutions

Benefits

  • Low, flat per-transaction cost
  • Automated disbursement & collection
  • NACHA / CTX file generation
  • Automated SFTP upload to banks
  • Real-time status tracking
  • Multi-bank support

Challenges → solutions

  • Returns & retries → status tracking
  • Reconciliation → unique-ID matching
  • Compliance → NACHA formatting
  • Scale → batch file automation
  • Settlement lag → predictable 1–3 day windows
The hard parts of ACH are returns handling and reconciliation. The platform's answer is unique-ID matching and NACHA-formatted batch files uploaded automatically via SFTP, with multi-bank support so a large portfolio isn't tied to one institution.
