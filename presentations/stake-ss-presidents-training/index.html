Teaching in the Savior's Way

All things center on Jesus Christ

"I am the way, the truth, and the life." — John 14:6

The Four Principles of Christlike Teaching

Love Those You Teach
See each learner the way Christ sees them — with infinite worth and infinite potential
Focus on Jesus Christ
He is the center of every lesson. Every discussion, every question, every story — points back to Him
Teach the Doctrine
Clearly, simply, powerfully — avoiding complexity that distracts from conversion
Invite Diligent Learning
Create space for the Spirit to teach. Ask inspired questions. Issue meaningful invitations to act
Every principle converges at one point — Deepened conversion to Jesus Christ — through Teach by the Spirit
Walk through each card together. The key insight: it is not any one principle alone, but all of them pointing toward deepened conversion to Jesus Christ. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Which of these four is your ward's greatest strength? Which is the greatest growth opportunity? Follow-up: "How would you help a teacher who loves their class but teaches stories instead of doctrine?"
This is our guiding resource — Teaching in the Savior's Way. The Venn diagram on the right is the whole framework in one image. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Which circle do your ward teachers spend the most time in? Which do they neglect most? He Is the Model

"Come, follow me."
— Luke 18:22
  • He knew those He taught — their names, struggles, potential
  • He asked questions that invited reflection and revelation
  • He used simple stories that anyone could remember
  • He always invited action — "go and do," "arise," "follow me"
This image is from the Book of Mormon film — the resurrected Christ among the Nephites. He came TO them. He didn't wait for them to come to Him. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Who in your ward needs the Savior to "come to them" right now through Sunday School? What would that actually look like?

Search Questions

A purposefully crafted, open-ended question that initiates an investigation, prompts deeper thinking, and guides students to discover information rather than just recalling it.
How to Use
  • Ask before reading the scripture passage
  • Keep it open-ended — no single "right" answer
  • Let students sit with it as they read
  • Invite discovery, not recitation
Examples
  • "As you read, look for what this teaches about God's character."
  • "What does Nephi do that surprises you — and why?"
  • "What question would you want to ask the author of this verse?"
Practice Together
As you listen to Elder Bednar's words, allow the Spirit to inspire your mind with search questions you could ponder or ask your own children and class members.
Watch: Elder Bednar on Search Questions
The difference between a search question and a recall question: Recall: "What did Alma say?" — students look for the answer. Search: "What does this reveal about how God sees us?" — students think and discover. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: When did a teacher's question change the way you read a scripture? What made it powerful? Give presidencies 2 minutes to craft one search question together, then share.

The Power of Ministering to the One

Elder Peter M. Johnson  ·  General Conference, October 2025
"As we live the doctrine of Christ and minister to the one in ways that lead to the house of the Lord, we will find answers and experience spiritual renewal."
— Elder Peter M. Johnson
Minister Without Judgment
Christ treated the Samaritan woman with dignity — she then brought others to believe in Him
Lift, Don't Just Give
Peter and John lifted the lame beggar into the temple — he left "walking, leaping, praising God"
Simply Love
Sometimes the most powerful ministry is repeating: "I love you" — until they can feel the Spirit again
Elder Johnson shared his own faith crisis — his companion Kevin Vick ministered by simply saying "I love you" over and over until Johnson could pray and feel the Spirit again. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Who in your ward needs someone to just sit with them and say "I love you" right now — not a lesson, not a program — just presence and love? The key insight: ministering leads to the temple. That's the destination. Every act of reaching out is an invitation back to the house of the Lord.
