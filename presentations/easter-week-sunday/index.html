Palm Sunday & Triumphal Entry
An Easter Week Art Display · Brad Young
Day 1 of Easter Week. Jesus enters Jerusalem on a donkey to the jubilant cries of "Hosanna!" The crowd spreads palm branches and garments on the road before Him. This painting depicts the Triumphal Entry — Jesus riding humbly on a donkey into Jerusalem while jubilant crowds wave palm branches and spread their garments in the road. The contrast is profound: the King of Kings arriving not in a war chariot, but on a colt.
"Tell ye the daughter of Sion, Behold, thy King cometh unto thee, meek, and sitting upon an ass, and a colt the foal of an ass…

"And a very great multitude spread their garments in the way; others cut down branches from the trees, and strawed them in the way. And the multitudes that went before, and that followed, cried, saying, Hosanna to the Son of David: Blessed is he that cometh in the name of the Lord; Hosanna in the highest."
— Matthew 21:5, 8–9
The prophecy of Zechariah 9:9 was fulfilled exactly: the King arrived meek, on a donkey. "Hosanna" means "Save us now." The crowd's cry was a plea and a praise combined — they recognized their Deliverer even as they misunderstood the nature of His deliverance.
The crowd who welcomed Jesus would, within five days, call for His crucifixion. Yet this moment of joy was real — a preview of the eternal welcome that awaits those who come to Him. He entered the city knowing what lay ahead, and came anyway.
"And Jesus entered into Jerusalem, and into the temple: and when he had looked round about upon all things, and now the eventide was come, he went out unto Bethany with the twelve."
— Mark 11:11
After the triumphant entry, Jesus quietly surveyed the temple — taking in what He found there. Then He withdrew to Bethany with His Apostles. The King had come to His house. The cleansing would come the next day.
