Easter Week · Day 7

Saturday

Teaching in the Spirit World
An Easter Week Art Display · Brad Young
Day 7 of Easter Week — the Saturday between the crucifixion and the Resurrection. While His body lay in the tomb, the spirit of Jesus Christ went to the spirit world and organized the preaching of the gospel to those who had died without it. President Joseph F. Smith's vision in D&C 138 gives us an extraordinary window into this sacred day. Press S to open speaker notes.
This painting depicts the Savior's ministry in the spirit world — a vast congregation of those who had waited, some since the days of Noah, to hear the gospel. The Atonement reaches beyond death itself. No generation is forgotten. No soul is beyond His reach.
"By which also he went and preached unto the spirits in prison… For this cause was the gospel preached also to them that are dead, that they might be judged according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit…

"And the saints rejoiced in their redemption, and bowed the knee and acknowledged the Son of God as their Redeemer and Deliverer from death and the chains of hell. Their countenances shone, and the radiance from the presence of the Lord rested upon them, and they sang praises unto his holy name."
— 1 Peter 3:19; 4:6 · D&C 138:15–16
D&C 138 is one of the most tender revelations in all of scripture — the veil pulled back on what Christ did during those hours in the tomb. He was not idle. He was busy with the greatest rescue mission ever undertaken. The gospel is truly for all generations.
