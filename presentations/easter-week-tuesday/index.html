Easter Week · Day 3

Tuesday

Teaches Parables
An Easter Week Art Display · Brad Young
Day 3 of Easter Week. Tuesday was the busiest teaching day of the Savior's final week. He taught in parables — the Ten Virgins, the Talents, the Sheep and the Goats — each one a window into the coming kingdom and our readiness for it. Press S to open speaker notes.
The ten virgins waiting for the bridegroom. Five had oil in their lamps; five did not. When the bridegroom came at midnight, those without oil found the door shut. The parable is a call to personal, non-transferable preparation.
"Then shall the kingdom of heaven be likened unto ten virgins, which took their lamps, and went forth to meet the bridegroom…

"Then all those virgins arose, and trimmed their lamps. And the foolish said unto the wise, Give us of your oil; for our lamps are gone out. But the wise answered…Go ye rather to them that sell, and buy for yourselves…

"And while they went to buy, the bridegroom came; and they that were ready went in with him to the marriage: and the door was shut."
— Matthew 25:1, 7–10
"Give us of your oil" — but oil cannot be borrowed. Conversion, testimony, spiritual preparation cannot be transferred at the last moment. The parable asks each of us: Is my lamp trimmed? Is it full? Are you ready now, not just intending to be ready?
The master entrusts his servants with talents according to their ability — five, two, and one. Two double what they were given. One buries his in fear. The parable is about faithful use of what we have been given — not the amount, but the faithfulness.
"For the kingdom of heaven is as a man travelling into a far country, who called his own servants, and delivered unto them his goods. And unto one he gave five talents, to another two, and to another one; to every man according to his several ability…

"After a long time the lord of those servants cometh, and reckoneth with them…

"His lord said unto him, Well done, thou good and faithful servant: thou hast been faithful over a few things, I will make thee ruler over many things."
— Matthew 25:14–15, 19, 21
"Well done, thou good and faithful servant." This is what the Lord seeks from us — not spectacular achievement, but faithful use of what we have. The servant who buried his talent feared the master and acted from fear, not love. Fear is a poor foundation for discipleship.
The sheep and the goats — the great judgment parable. The Son of Man on His throne separates the nations. The sheep inherit the kingdom; the goats are sent away. The dividing line is not doctrine stated but charity practiced.
"When the Son of man shall come in his glory…then shall he sit upon the throne of his glory: And before him shall be gathered all nations: and he shall separate them one from another, as a shepherd divideth his sheep from the goats…

"Come, ye blessed of my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world: For I was an hungred, and ye gave me meat: I was thirsty, and ye gave me drink: I was a stranger, and ye took me in: Naked, and ye clothed me: I was sick, and ye visited me: I was in prison, and ye came unto me."
— Matthew 25:31–32, 34–36
"Inasmuch as ye have done it unto one of the least of these my brethren, ye have done it unto me." The Savior identifies completely with the suffering. To serve the poor, the sick, the stranger, the imprisoned — is to serve Him. Discipleship is not theoretical; it has a face and an address.
