Easter Week · Day 2

Monday

Cleanses Temple · Heals & Teaches
An Easter Week Art Display · Brad Young
Day 2 of Easter Week. Monday brought three defining acts: Jesus cleansed the temple of money changers, healed the blind and lame who came to Him there, and taught the people who gathered daily in the temple courts. Press S to open speaker notes.
Jesus drives out the merchants and money changers from the temple — overturning tables, scattering coins. This is not a moment of loss of control, but of righteous authority. The Son of God reclaiming His Father's house.
"And Jesus went into the temple of God, and cast out all them that sold and bought in the temple, and overthrew the tables of the moneychangers, and the seats of them that sold doves,

"And said unto them, It is written, My house shall be called the house of prayer; but ye have made it a den of thieves."
— Matthew 21:12–13
The temple was meant to be a place where all nations could come to pray. Instead, commerce had crowded out worship. The quotation from Isaiah and Jeremiah combined into a single indictment — He was declaring what the house was for and what it had become.
Immediately after cleansing the temple, the blind and lame came to Jesus there — and He healed them. The temple, purified, became immediately a place of healing. The chief priests saw it and were indignant. The children cried "Hosanna."
"And the blind and the lame came to him in the temple; and he healed them."
— Matthew 21:14
In seven words, Matthew captures the simplicity and completeness of Christ's healing ministry. They came. He healed them. No ceremony, no delay. The temple, cleansed, became a hospital — exactly what a house of the Lord is for.
Day after day Jesus taught in the temple. Luke 19:47 tells us the chief priests, scribes, and leaders sought to destroy Him — but they could not, because all the people hung on every word He spoke. The people were His shield, and His word was His weapon.
"And he taught daily in the temple. But the chief priests and the scribes and the chief of the people sought to destroy him, and could not find what they might do: for all the people were very attentive to hear him."
— Luke 19:47
The opposition was real — plotting, calculating. Yet it was held at bay by something unexpected: ordinary people who simply wanted to hear Him. The people's hunger for His word was a form of protection. So it is today — when we gather to hear Him, something of darkness is kept at bay.
